Background

Solar Citizens is an independent, community-based organization that brings millions of solar owners and supporters together to take the next step and keep growing Australian renewable energy. From the power of people movement who stands for the rights of solar owners and sensible clean energy policies, Solar Citizens has achieved victories for many times in terms of government policies (Solar Citizens, n.d.).

Research

Solar power is energy created by the heat and light from the sun, which can convert into electricity in order to heat the air, water, or to create solar fuels like hydrogen. At the moment, there are two types of solar power technology: Solar photovoltaic, and Solar thermal.

Solar photovoltaic converts sunlight directly through semiconductor cell or solar PV cell. Solar PV panels are currently the most popular type of solar energy.

Solar thermal converts sunlight into heat and can be used to various purposes such as creating steam to make electricity or used to drive refrigeration cycle with solar-based cooling (Australian Renewable Energy Agency, 2020).

Australian continent has highest solar energy resources per square meter more than any other countries in the world. Australia receives an average of 58 million PJ (petajoule) of solar radiation per year, which is around 10,000 times larger than its total energy consumption (Geoscience Australia, n.d.).

In 2016 - 17, Solar PV generated 3.1% of Australia’s electricity with more than two million of Australian households (21%) that have rooftop solar PV installed (Australian Renewable Energy Agency, 2020).

In 2019, small-scale solar PV was generated 22.3% of total clean energy and produced 5.3% of Australia’s total electricity. There were 287,504 rooftop solar installations, and more than 2.5 million Australian households are using benefits of rooftop solar right now (Clean Energy Council, n.d.).

While electricity prices have been continued to skyrocket, solar panel has become more affordable with the price falling more than 75% since 2009, and 500 times cheaper that the first solar cells produced in mid-1950s. Although there were less than 100,000 households installed with solar panel in 2008, there were more than two million rooftops solar installed in the last decade making Australia a number one in the world when it comes to rooftop solar. This fact makes the future of solar energy in Australia looking very bright and strong (Solar Citizens, n.d.).

User Profiles

The target users for this website would be Australian citizens who are interested to use solar power by installing solar PV on their rooftop for a cheaper electricity cost in the long run. Also, those who want to support the idea of using clean renewable energy in Australia especially citizens who live in South Australia, Northern Territory, and Tasmania area because according to reseach these areas are not having much solar PV installations yet compare to other states such as New South Wales, Australian Capital Territory, Victoria, Queensland, and Western Australia (Solar Feeds, 2019).

The website needs to give information about what is solar power and how it can help them saving electricity cost in the long run and nation as a whole. Also, it should be designed for users to navigate freely and make them want to support the organization either by volunteering or donation.

Ideation

To design the website for Solar Citizens organization, there are three stages of iterations taking place.

  • 1st iteration is a rough idea of the website showing from landing page to navigation bars and where it leads to.
  • 2nd iteration is a self-review of the first iteration to see what works, what needs to change or add.
  • 3rd iteration is the final plan for the website showing from landing page to navigation bars and all the pages it would lead users to.

Organization Info. Page

There are four sketches I did for an Organization Info. Page. It was quite challenging for me to design this page because I think most of my sketches end up too minimalistic. However, in the end, I decided to put things together in a balanced manners that I am satisfied with.

This Final Sketch of Organization Info. Page would mainly rely on the clicking to see the information inside. By clicking the years, the box would show up a brief summary of the year and the annual reports that users can click again to download it. This annual report is necessary for credibility purpose of the organization.


Campaigns Page

Out of the three sketches for Campaigns Page, the first sketch seems quite weak in the design part in my opinion. I actually quite like the 3rd design, but the problem with it is that it takes too much space on the webpage. Therefore, I choose to mainly focus on refining the 2nd sketch.

After doing some refining on the 2nd sketch, there are design elements that I decided to add to the Final Sketch of Campaigns Page such as

  • The arrow representing the beginning to the future ahead
  • using different colors for past, current, and upcoming for easier visual.
  • More clicking interactive, when the users click on the date, numbers of campaigns will show up. After clicking the name of the campaigns, little box will pop up and let the users read the brief explanation of the campaigns.

Get in Touch Page

From these three sketches of Get in Touch Page I decided to do more refining on the second and the third sketches because I really like the balance of putting information on two sides of the webpage, and the idea of connection in the visual design part.

Taking the design elements that I like from the previous two sketches, the Final Sketch of Get in Touch Page is visually presents by the handshake picture.
